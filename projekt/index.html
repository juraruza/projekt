About The Simpsons

The Simpsons is an American animated sitcom created by Matt Groening. It premiered on December 17, 1989, and has since become one of the longest-running television series in history. The show follows the lives of the Simpson family in the fictional town of Springfield, offering satirical commentary on American culture, society, and television.

About The Author

Matt Groening is an American cartoonist, writer, producer, and animator. He is best known for creating The Simpsons and the comic strip Life in Hell. Born in 1954 in Portland, Oregon, Groening has won numerous awards for his work in animation, including multiple Emmy Awards. His distinctive style and satirical humor have made him a prominent figure in the entertainment industry.
